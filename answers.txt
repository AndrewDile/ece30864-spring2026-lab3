(a)
Pros
1. Breaks down a project into achievable parts and stages so that they can be organized and tracked independently, as well as released incrementally.
2. Helps quantify how long projects or parts of projects may take, and allows all to access and view the efficiency of teams and members.
Cons
1. Takes a while to set up, especially if the project is already underway, and can be hard to move from big deadlines to smaller sprint ideology.
2. Requires everyone to follow the standards, and requires a high level of discipline to manage the constant communication and upkeep.

(b)
Scrum Manager - responsible for all scrum related tasks, including JIRA, and facilitating springs and meetings.
Product Owner - responsible for setting direction for the team, and handles releases and stakeholder/customer management.

(c)
1. Sprint Planning Meeting
    a) Determine what to complete in the coming sprint.
    b) Determine story points and assign stories to teams.
2. Daily Scrum/Standup Meeting
    a) Discuss work completed.
    b) Discuss work plan for the day and any blockers.
3. Sprint Retrospective Meeting
    a) Discuss work completed.
    b) Move incomplete work to backlog.

(d)
Epic - Largest body of work that is broken down into stories.
Story - Software requirement expressed in a few short sentences.
Bug - Smallet piece of work, typically to fix code that isn't working as intended.
Task - A unit of work that contributes towards completing a story.
